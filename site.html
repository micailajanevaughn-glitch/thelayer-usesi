Operationalizing the Digital Investment.
Bridging the gap between SSG strategy and branch reality.

USESI has built a world-class digital engine through the Shared Services Group. The opportunity now is ensuring that engine powers the operational reality of 14 regional companies — from the lighting desks at Wiedenbach Brown to the branch teams at Electrical Wholesalers, Walters, and Monarch.

To the USESI Executive Team

The tools are in place. The translation is the missing piece.

USESI has already made the critical investment. Through the Shared Services Group, the digital engine is built. The AI licenses are rolling out. The infrastructure is in place. That is the right move — and it positions USESI ahead of most organizations in this industry.

But there is a growing Translation Gap between the parent company's digital strategy and the daily operational reality of the 14 regional companies. Branch managers and lighting specialists are focused on protecting margin, managing complex projects, and moving deals from spec to submittal. They don't have the bandwidth to figure out how a generic AI tool fits into a lighting department workflow. Meanwhile, the most experienced people in the organization are retiring — and their decades of accumulated expertise are leaving the building with them.

I am not an IT consultant. I am a 20-year lighting veteran who understands exactly how a deal moves from initial specification through value engineering, submittal, award, and delivery. My role is to serve as the Strategic Bridge between SSG's technical rollout and the operational ground truth of USESI's regional companies — ensuring that the investment already made actually delivers at the branch level.

The three outcomes this engagement drives: AI tools that lighting teams actually use. A Mentorship Architecture that captures veteran expertise before it retires. And a Visibility Layer that gives the parent company intelligence across all 14 companies that currently exists nowhere.

Three Strategic Outcomes

What this engagement delivers — and for whom.

I
AI Adoption That Actually Lands
Operationalizing the SSG investment at the branch level

USESI has built the digital engine. This pillar ensures the engine powers the front line. Working alongside SSG's technical team, I serve as the lighting-specific liaison — translating AI tools into workflows that estimators, project managers, and sales teams actually use. The goal is to move from "having licenses" to "lighting-aware automation" that saves time and protects margin at every branch.

  • Implement governance frameworks before deployment — what AI can do autonomously, what needs human review, what stays human
  • Translate lighting operational workflows into technical requirements that internal teams can build against
  • Validate AI outputs against professional standards — the expertise check that technical teams alone cannot perform
  • Move AI from disconnected regional experiments to a unified corporate asset
II
The Mentorship Architecture
Codifying the USESI Brain Trust before it retires

The greatest risk to USESI's long-term growth is not technology — it is the expertise leak. Every month, 30-year veterans retire and take decades of uncaptured lighting knowledge with them. This pillar builds a structured 1-2 year mentorship program — not a course library, not a one-time onboarding — a living program that pairs new hires with curriculum, with experienced professionals, and with the specific knowledge of how lighting works inside USESI's ecosystem.

  • Codify veteran "gut feel" — spec strategies, manufacturer relationships, pricing instincts — into a repeatable training engine
  • Design a hybrid curriculum using Rocky Mountain Lighting Academy and IES courses as a foundation, customized for USESI's operational model
  • Build the pairing structure — new hires connected to experienced professionals with competency benchmarks and milestone tracking
  • Build the talent pipeline now — before the gap becomes a hiring crisis
III
The Visibility Layer
Capturing pre-PO intelligence across all 14 companies

Right now, the most important data in a lighting deal happens before the purchase order is placed — and it is invisible to the parent company. Manufacturer performance, margin patterns, rep relationships, spec-to-award conversion — all of it lives in people's heads or in disconnected branch systems. This pillar builds the data layer that surfaces that intelligence across all 14 companies as a byproduct of work already happening.

  • Design mechanisms to capture pre-PO intelligence — the conversations, negotiations, and spec decisions that drive margin
  • Build visibility into manufacturer performance, rep relationships, and procurement patterns across all 14 companies
  • Surface pipeline and margin intelligence at the parent level — not as a reporting burden, but as a byproduct of how work flows
  • Give leadership the ability to see across the full portfolio and act on what they see
