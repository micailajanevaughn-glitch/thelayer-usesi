The Problem

Lighting is where
the translation breaks down.

The spec process doesn't move like the rest of the electrical portfolio. A project lives in conversations, submittals, and relationships for months before it touches a system.

Organizations are investing heavily in infrastructure that misses — because no one translated the actual work first.

The Gap

The decisions that drive margin
happen before anyone opens a system.

Which manufacturers delivered, performed, and negotiated their best terms. Which procurement model closed most profitably. Whether the rep relationship protected the deal or complicated it.

That intelligence exists — it's living in people's heads instead of anywhere it can be used. And that's only the middle of the problem.

The Role

This isn't one company's overhead.
It's infrastructure for the whole portfolio.

USESI has 14 companies. The lighting gap isn't unique to one of them. The cost of building this right is shared. The return compounds across all of them.

What's needed is someone who sits between the systems being built and the operations they need to serve.

What Changes

Two things that make
everything else work.

01

A knowledge and training system built around how lighting actually gets sold, specified, and delivered — so institutional knowledge becomes searchable and repeatable instead of walking out the door with the next retirement.

02

A data layer built around the activity that's already happening — so pipeline, margin, and project status surface as a byproduct of doing the work, not as a separate reporting burden. Leadership sees across the portfolio. Sales people see what's worth chasing.
